Cross-floor forking — why some tiers split into dedicated subsets

Screw, Steel Pipe, and Cable each feed two different downstream floors in a ratio that isn't a clean half or third (e.g. Screw: 240/min to Heavy Modular Frame vs 180/min to Reinforced Iron Plate). A single shared manifold can't legally fork that way on a solid belt, so instead of pooling identical machines and attempting an uneven split, each destination gets its own dedicated machine subset from the start — e.g. 4 Screw Constructors run at 150% exclusively for the HMF-bound stream, a separate 3 run at 150% exclusively for the RIP-bound stream. Both subsets hit their target exactly and there's no splitter involved at all.

Iron Ingot and Copper Ingot avoid this problem entirely by co-location: their two consumers (Iron Rod+Iron Plate; Wire+Copper Sheet) share the same floor, so the fork is an internal same-floor feed, not a belt split.
