Layout & routing decisions

Circuit Board and Computer share Floor 2 specifically so the 16/min portion of Circuit Board's output that feeds Computer can be an internal same-floor feed — Circuit Board's total output (26/min) doesn't split evenly (16:10 is not a clean half or third), so keeping the uneven portion internal avoids needing an explicit splitter/merger network for a solid belt item. Only the clean 10/min direct-to-ACU portion leaves via the output strip.

Stator and Automated Wiring share Floor 1 for the same reason — Stator's full 10/min output feeds Automated Wiring entirely (a 1:1 internal feed, not a split at all).

Automated Wiring and Circuit Board both need more than one machine because their required clock (400% and 347% respectively) exceeds the single-machine 250% cap — duplicating to 2 machines each brings the per-machine clock back under cap (200% and 173.33%).

⚠ Wire demand (544/min) exceeds even Mk.4 belt cap (480/min) — Floor 1's Wire intake needs two parallel belts, not one trunk.
