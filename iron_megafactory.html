Ore & miner routing

240 ore/min feeds LB 0b, which uses a 7-splitter binary tree (SPL1–SPL7) to distribute it into 8 equal 30/min belts, one per smelter on Floor 1.

The ore trunk from the miner(s) to LB 0b requires a Mk.3 belt (240/min, under the 270/min cap).

// Miner count, node purity, and clock speed are not recorded in any floor-plan file (they only show "← miners" on the input strip) — left as TBD in the summary bar above pending confirmation against the actual save.
Ingot split & routing

Floor 1's 8 smelters each output 30/min ingots via individual belt lifts to LB 1b. LB 1b uses 4 direct pass-through belts (P1–P4, 30/min each, cols 2–5) plus 4× 2-way splitters (SPL1–SPL4, cols 6–9) that each halve 30/min into two 15/min belts (R1–R8).

Floor 2 receives 120/min ingots on 4 belts (30/min each, cols 2–5) feeding plate constructors, and 120/min ingots on 8 belts (15/min each, cols 6–9... reindexed on Floor 2 as R1–R8) feeding rod constructors.

// 240/min splits cleanly into two 120/min tracks (4×30 direct + 8×15 via splitters) — no uneven-split workaround needed.
Rod manifold & routing (LB 2b)

Floor 2's 8 rod constructors produce 120/min total, arriving at LB 2b as a single 120/min trunk (SPL1) that splits into a screw-side branch (SPL2, 60/min) and an MF/Rotor-side branch (SPL5, 60/min).

The screw side further splits through SPL3/SPL4 into 6× 10/min belts to Floor 3's screw constructors. The MF/Rotor side splits through SPL6 (30/min → 2× 15/min to LB 4b for MF) and SPL7 (30/min → store + 2× 10/min merged at MRG1 into 20/min → Floor 4 Rotors).

// Rod trunk to LB 2b runs Mk.3 (120/min, well under the 270/min cap) per lb2b_rod_manifold.html's own belt-requirement box — do not downgrade to Mk.2, which would leave zero headroom.
Plate, screw & rod routing (LB 3b)

Floor 3's 6 screw constructors produce 240/min, entering LB 3b's screw network (SSPL1–4, SMRG1–2) which splits it into 60/min to RIP-A, 60/min to RIP-B, 100/min to Rotors, and 20/min to storage.

Floor 2's 80/min plates enter LB 3b's separate plate network (PSPL1–3, PMRG1) and split into 30/min to RIP-A, 30/min to RIP-B, and 20/min to storage. The 20/min rod belt from LB 2b (bound for Floor 4 Rotors) passes through this floor unmodified.

// Two independent networks share this floor (plate cols 1–2, screw cols 4–8) per the combined-network-floor convention — see the floor file's own node colour key for both.
RIP & Rotors assembly (Floor 4)

Two RIP assemblers run at 90% clock (4.5/min each, 9/min total), each consuming 27/min plates and 54/min screws. One Rotors assembler runs at 100% (4/min), consuming 20/min rods and 100/min screws.

Floor 4's outputs (9/min RIP, 4/min Rotors) descend to LB 4b, which further splits RIP into MF1/MF2/Smart Plating branches and Rotors into Smart Plating/storage branches.

// RIP assemblers running at 90% (rather than 100% like every other machine here) was verified by trace-test as internally consistent — 4.5/min output and 27/54 plate/screw consumption all recompute correctly from the recipe at that clock speed. Not a defect, just an unusual deliberate choice.
