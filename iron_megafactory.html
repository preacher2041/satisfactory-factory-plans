Ore & miner routing

240 ore/min feeds LB 0b, which uses a 7-splitter binary tree (SPL1–SPL7) to distribute it into 8 equal 30/min belts, one per smelter on Floor 1.

The ore trunk from the miner(s) to LB 0b requires a Mk.3 belt (240/min, under the 270/min cap).

// Miner count, node purity, and clock speed are not recorded in any floor-plan file (they only show "← miners" on the input strip) — left as TBD in the summary bar above pending confirmation against the actual save.
Ingot split & routing

Floor 1's 8 smelters each output 30/min ingots. Two internal merger trees combine Sm1–4 into a single 120/min "Rod-Ingot Trunk" and Sm5–8 into a single 120/min "Plate-Ingot Trunk" — a genuinely new addition to this floor, since it previously had no internal nodes.

The Rod-Ingot Trunk feeds LB 1b directly (adjacent floor), which splits it via a 3-level tree into 8× 15/min belts for Floor 2's rod constructors. The Plate-Ingot Trunk bypasses LB 1b and Floor 2 via an external lift to reach LB 2b, which splits it via a 2-level tree into 4× 30/min belts for Floor 3's plate constructors.

// 240/min splits cleanly into two 120/min tracks (4×30 merged + 8×15 merged) — no uneven-split workaround needed. The Plate-Ingot Trunk's bypass past LB 1b/Floor 2 is an accepted tradeoff of keeping every floor single-purpose.
Rod manifold & routing (LB 3b)

Floor 2's 8 rod constructors produce 120/min total, merged on-floor into a single trunk (via a new 7-merger binary tree) that bypasses LB 2b and Floor 3 to reach LB 3b as one 120/min belt (SPL1), splitting into a screw-side branch (SPL2, 60/min) and an MF/Rotor-side branch (SPL5, 60/min).

The screw side further splits through SPL3/SPL4 into 6× 10/min belts to Floor 4's screw constructors. The MF/Rotor side splits through SPL6 (30/min → 2× 15/min, bypassing forward to Floor 7 for MF) and SPL7 (30/min → store + 2× 10/min merged at MRG1 into 20/min, bypassing forward to Floor 6 Rotors).

// Rod trunk to LB 3b runs Mk.3 (120/min, well under the 270/min cap) per lb3b_rod_manifold.html's own belt-requirement box. The MF-bound rods bypass 7 intervening floors to reach Floor 7 — the longest bypass in the factory, clearly labeled on both ends.
Screw routing (LB 4b)

Floor 4's 6 screw constructors produce 240/min, entering LB 4b's network (SPL1–4, MRG1–2) which splits it into 60/min to RIP-A, 60/min to RIP-B, 100/min to Floor 6 Rotors (bypass), and 20/min to storage.

// Adjacent to Floor 4 (no bypass needed on the input side); its Rotors-bound output bypasses past Floor 5/LB 5c to reach Floor 6.
Plate routing (LB 4c)

Floor 3's 4 plate constructors produce 80/min total, merged on-floor into a single trunk (via a new 3-merger tree) that bypasses LB 3b, Floor 4, and LB 4b to reach LB 4c, which splits it (PSPL1–3, PMRG1) into 30/min to RIP-A, 30/min to RIP-B, and 20/min to storage.

// Previously combined with screw routing on one floor (old LB 3b); now isolated onto its own dedicated floor, mirroring the same split applied to Floor 2/3 and Floor 5/6.
RIP assembly (Floor 5)

Two RIP assemblers run at 90% clock (4.5/min each, 9/min total), each consuming 27/min plates and 54/min screws, but are supplied 30/min plates + 60/min screws by LB 4c/LB 4b's fixed splitter trees.

Floor 5's output (9/min RIP) descends to LB 5c, which splits RIP into MF1/MF2/Smart Plating/storage branches.

// LB 4b/LB 4c are fixed-ratio splitter/merger trees, not manifolds — the 3/min plate + 6/min screw per machine (6/12 combined) that the 90%-clock RIP assemblers don't consume has no explicit drain route captured on any floor file, unlike Floor 7's equivalent rod surplus which is routed to storage. Documented as known headroom on floor5_rip.html pending a decision to either re-clock the RIP assemblers to 100% or redesign the upstream splits — flagged by trace-test, not fabricated as resolved.
Rotor assembly (Floor 6)

One Rotors assembler runs at 100% (4/min), consuming 20/min rods (bypassed from LB 3b) and 100/min screws (bypassed from LB 4b). Floor 6's output (4/min Rotors) descends to LB 6b, which splits Rotors into Smart Plating/storage branches.
