Miners-only overclock & manifold routing

Ore comes from 2 Impure iron nodes rather than Normal ones. A Miner Mk.2's Impure-node base rate is only 60/min at 100% (0.5× purity modifier on the Mk.2's 120/min default), so reaching the same 150/min per miner that this factory's downstream floors are built around requires the full 250% clock (3 shards each, 6 total) — the 3-shard ceiling. Every downstream machine (Smelters, Rod Constructors, Screw Constructors) still runs at 100%, so total output is unchanged (still ore-limited at 1200 screws/min): 5 Smelters, 10 Rod Constructors, and 15 Screw Constructors per line.

The two ore nodes remain fully parallel, symmetric lines (Line A, Line B) end to end, and stay fully separate all the way through — Line A's rods never mix with Line B's; each line gets its own dedicated screw floor. Floor 1 (Smelters) uses a dedicated input splitter + output merger per machine, chained machine-to-machine. Floor 2 (Rods) uses single-level merger trees (MRG1-10, no splitters) to combine constructor outputs into per-line streams.

// Trade-off vs. the fully-overclocked version: 78 shards → 6 shards, at the cost of 26 → 62 total production machines and 3 → 5 floors (screw production alone needed 2 extra floors since 15 constructors/line don't fit on one floor even stacked). Impure nodes need the full 250% clock (6 shards) to hit 150/min per miner, vs. only 125% (2 shards) if the same factory ran on Normal nodes instead — Impure's 60/min base is half a Normal node's 120/min, so it needs double the overclock percentage for the same output.
Screw floors: whole-line routing & the small LB

Floors 3 and 4 each carry one entire line's rod supply (150/min), rather than blending both lines across both floors — Floor 3 is 100% Line A, Floor 4 is 100% Line B, with zero cross-mixing. This means the rod trunk never has to be split between floors at all.

Within each floor, a small genuine load-balancer divides that floor's 150/min into 100/min and 50/min: a 3-way splitter produces exact thirds (50+50+50 — a valid splitter split, not a fudge), and a merger recombines two of those thirds into 100/min. That feeds a 10-Constructor row; the remaining 50/min third feeds a separate 5-Constructor row. 100+50=150 ✓, and 10+5=15 Constructors per floor, all with their own dedicated per-machine splitter+merger chain (like Floor 1).
