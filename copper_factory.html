Miner & ore routing

A single Miner Mk.2 on a normal copper node overclocked to 150% produces 300 ore/min, consuming one power shard. This is the only clock speed deviation in the factory — all smelters and constructors run at 100%.

The 300/min ore trunk requires a Mk.3 belt. At LB 0b a recirculating splitter tree distributes 300/min equally across 10 smelters: SPL1 splits 150+150, each half feeds a merger which combines the trunk with a 30/min return loop to produce a clean 180/min that then splits into three 3-way splitters feeding 5 smelters each side.

// The recirculating tree avoids the impossible 10-way binary split and produces perfectly balanced 30/min feeds to every smelter.
Ingot split & routing

Floor 1 outputs 300 ingots/min via two separate belt lifts on the exterior wall. The 180/min wire feed (Sm1–Sm6) rises to LB 1b on a Mk.3 belt; the 120/min sheet feed (Sm7–Sm10) bypasses LB 1b entirely and rises directly to LB 2b on a Mk.2 belt (at cap — do not downgrade).

LB 1b is a pure pass-through — the 6 ingot belts enter one side and exit the other, routing from the output side of Floor 1 to the input side of Floor 2. No splitting or merging occurs on this floor.

// The 300/min ingot split (180+120) is a 3:2 ratio not achievable with a single splitter. Two independent belt lifts from Floor 1 is the cleanest solution.
Wire routing & split

Floor 2 produces 180 wire/min (6 constructors × 30/min each). The 6 individual 30/min belts descend to LB 2b where they are paired into 3 mergers: WMRG1 (W1+W2=60/min → C1), WMRG2 (W3+W4=60/min → storage), WMRG3 (W5+W6=60/min → C2).

Each cable constructor on Floor 3 receives exactly 60/min wire, consuming all of it (2 wire/cycle × 30/min = 60/min). The 60/min wire to storage exits directly from WMRG2 to ground floor storage.

// 180/min splits cleanly into 3× 60/min — a simple equal 3-way that requires only mergers on LB 2b, no splitters needed on the wire side.
Sheet ingot routing

The 120/min sheet ingot track (Sm7–Sm10, 4× 30/min) rises directly from Floor 1 to LB 2b, sharing the floor with the wire network. Four splitters (SPL1–SPL4) each split 30/min into 3× 10/min, which are then recombined by 6 mergers (IMRG1–IMRG6) into 20/min feeds for each of the 6 sheet constructors on Floor 3.

Each sheet constructor consumes exactly 20/min (2 ingots/cycle × 10/min), producing 10 sheets/min for a total of 60 sheets/min.

// The 4-splitter 6-merger network on LB 2b converts 4× 30/min into 6× 20/min — an unequal distribution solved by cross-feeding adjacent splitter outputs into shared mergers.
