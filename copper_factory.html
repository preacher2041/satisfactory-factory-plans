Miner & ore routing

A single Miner Mk.2 on a normal copper node (base 120/min @ 100%) overclocked to 175% produces 210 ore/min, consuming 2 power shards (150% is the single-shard ceiling; 175% needs a second shard, still well under the 3-shard/250% max). This is the only clock speed deviation in the factory — all smelters and constructors run at 100%.

The 210/min ore trunk fits comfortably under the Mk.3 cap (270/min). At LB 0b a recirculating splitter tree distributes 210/min equally across 7 smelters using a pure binary structure: MRG1 merges the 210/min trunk with a 30/min return loop to produce a clean 240/min, which halves down through three levels (240→120→60→30) to reach 8 leaf branches. Seven of those become smelter feeds; the eighth recirculates back to MRG1 to sustain the loop.

// 7 is prime, so a plain binary tree can't divide it evenly either — recirculating exactly one leaf converts the impossible 7-way split into a clean run of 2-way halves, no thirds needed.
Ingot split & routing

Floor 1 outputs 210 ingots/min via individual exterior belt lifts, never merged into a single trunk. The 90/min wire feed (Sm1–Sm3, 3× Mk.1 belts) rises to LB 1b; the 120/min sheet feed (Sm7–Sm10, unchanged, 4× Mk.1 belts) bypasses LB 1b entirely and rises directly to LB 2b.

LB 1b now performs an actual split — 3 splitters (SPL1–SPL3), each an exact half (30→15+15), turn the 3× 30/min wire-track belts into the 6× 15/min feeds Floor 2's wire constructors actually consume.

// The 210/min ingot split (90+120) is a 3:4 ratio not achievable with a single splitter. Two independent belt lifts from Floor 1 is the cleanest solution.
// Resolved: wire's feed previously oversupplied Floor 2 2:1 (180/min supplied vs 90/min consumed, a 90/min surplus with no overflow branch on LB 1b — confirmed as-built, risked stalling 3 of the 6 wire-track smelters via backpressure). Fixed by cutting the wire track from 6 to 3 smelters (matching supply to the 90/min the 6 constructors actually need) and adding the 3 splitters above so each constructor still gets its own 15/min feed.
Wire routing & split

Floor 2 produces 180 wire/min (6 constructors × 30/min each). The 6 individual 30/min belts descend to LB 2b where they are paired into 3 mergers: WMRG1 (W1+W2=60/min → C1), WMRG2 (W3+W4=60/min → storage), WMRG3 (W5+W6=60/min → C2).

Each cable constructor on Floor 3 receives exactly 60/min wire, consuming all of it (2 wire/cycle × 30/min = 60/min). The 60/min wire to storage exits directly from WMRG2 to ground floor storage.

// 180/min splits cleanly into 3× 60/min — a simple equal 3-way that requires only mergers on LB 2b, no splitters needed on the wire side.
Sheet ingot routing

The 120/min sheet ingot track (Sm7–Sm10, 4× 30/min) rises directly from Floor 1 to LB 2b, sharing the floor with the wire network. Four splitters (SPL1–SPL4) each split 30/min into 3× 10/min, which are then recombined by 6 mergers (IMRG1–IMRG6) into 20/min feeds for each of the 6 sheet constructors on Floor 3.

Each sheet constructor consumes exactly 20/min (2 ingots/cycle × 10/min), producing 10 sheets/min for a total of 60 sheets/min.

// The 4-splitter 6-merger network on LB 2b converts 4× 30/min into 6× 20/min — an unequal distribution solved by cross-feeding adjacent splitter outputs into shared mergers.
