Miner & ore routing

A single Miner Mk.2 on a normal copper node overclocked to 150% produces 300 ore/min, consuming one power shard. This is the only clock speed deviation in the factory — all smelters and constructors run at 100%.

The 300/min ore trunk requires a Mk.3 belt. At LB 0b it is split 150+150 at a single splitter, then each 150/min half feeds an independent manifold of 5 smelters. Each smelter taps 30/min; the trunk decays 150→120→90→60→30→0 across the 5 machines. Mk.2 belts are sufficient for each manifold trunk (150/min < 270 cap).

// Two separate manifold chains avoids the impossible 10-way binary split. Each chain of 5 is independent — a single machine stalling affects only its half.
Ingot split & routing

Floor 1 outputs 300 ingots/min via two separate belt lifts on the exterior wall. The 180/min wire feed rises to LB 1b on a Mk.3 belt; the 120/min sheet feed bypasses LB 1b entirely and rises directly to LB 3b on a Mk.2 belt (at cap — do not downgrade).

LB 1b distributes 180/min to 6 wire constructors via manifold. The trunk decays 180→150→120→90→60→30→0 and requires a Mk.3 belt for the first three segments. LB 3b distributes 120/min to 6 sheet constructors via manifold. The trunk decays 120→100→80→60→40→20→0 — Mk.2 belt throughout but at cap on entry.

// The 300/min ingot split (180+120) is a 3:2 ratio — not achievable with a single splitter. Two independent belt lifts from Floor 1 is the cleanest solution; no splitter+merger network required.
Wire routing & split

Floor 2 produces 135 wire/min (6 constructors × 22.5/min). This belt descends to LB 2b where it is split: 120/min feeds the two cable constructors on Floor 3 (60/min each), and the remaining 15/min is routed to ground floor storage.

The wire trunk at 135/min exceeds the Mk.2 cap and requires a Mk.3 belt. The 120/min cable feed and 15/min storage run can use Mk.2 and Mk.1 belts respectively.

// 135/min cannot be split equally, so LB 2b uses a manifold tap approach: the two cable constructors tap 60/min each from the trunk, leaving 15/min to continue to storage. No splitter tree required.
